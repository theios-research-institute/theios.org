Codex Nexus Discovery Engine

A structured knowledge framework maintained by Theios Research Institute.
Hosted at codex.nexus.

About

The Codex Nexus Discovery Engine is a structured system of socio-cognitive laws and principles designed to map, test, and stress-validate patterns underlying human behavior, information, and emergent order.
This repository holds the machine-readable JSON nodes, validation scripts, and audit tools that ensure internal consistency and reproducibility.

Provenance & Signing

  • All commits and tags in this repository are cryptographically signed (git log --show-signature will verify).
  • Branch protection enforces signed commits only.
  • This ensures that the academic and scientific record is tamper-evident, auditable, and directly attributable to Neil Sargisian (Theios Research Institute).

Research Domains

Codex Nexus operates at the intersection of multiple disciplines. Each domain provides both constraints and affordances that shape the engine’s epistemic rigor:

  • Information Theory
    Studies of entropy, coding, and channel capacity guide how knowledge is measured, compressed, and transmitted within the engine.

  • Cognitive Science
    Offers models of human thought and perception, framing how discovery mechanisms mirror or diverge from natural cognition.

  • Complex Systems
    Provides the language of emergence, feedback, and nonlinearity, grounding the laws that capture self-organizing behaviors.

  • Epistemology
    Anchors the project in philosophical accounts of knowledge, justification, and belief revision.

  • Philosophy of Science
    Connects the framework to traditions of theory choice, scientific realism, and methodology.

  • Epistemic Logic
    Supplies formal languages for representing belief, knowledge, and inference, essential to structured discovery.

  • Computational Epistemology
    Merges epistemology with algorithmic methods, formalizing how agents acquire, test, and refine knowledge claims.

  • Knowledge Representation
    Informs how nodes and laws are encoded as machine-readable structures while retaining semantic depth.
