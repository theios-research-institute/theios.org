Privacy Policy

Theios Research Institute, Inc.

Last Updated: January 26, 2026

Summary: We respect your privacy. We collect minimal data, use Google Analytics for website improvement, don't sell personal information, and you can opt out of tracking at any time.

1. Introduction

Theios Research Institute, Inc. ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our websites (including theios.org and related domains) and use our services.

This policy applies to all our digital services, research platforms, and educational resources.

2. Information We Collect

Automatically Collected Information

When you visit our websites, we automatically collect certain information through Google Analytics, including:

  • Usage Data: Pages visited, time spent, click patterns
  • Device Information: Browser type, operating system, screen resolution
  • Location Data: General geographic location (country/city level)
  • Referral Information: Which website directed you to us
  • Technical Data: IP address, session duration, bounce rate

Information You Provide

We may collect information you voluntarily provide, such as:

  • Contact Information: When you email us at research@theios.org
  • Research Collaboration: Academic credentials for partnership discussions
  • Feedback: Comments, suggestions, or error reports
  • Citation Requests: Information needed for proper academic attribution

Commitment to Truth Pledge

When you sign the Commitment to Truth, we collect:

  • Full Name: Required to sign the pledge
  • Email Address: Required for confirmation and optional annual renewal reminders
  • Signup Date: For anniversary tracking
  • Public Ledger Consent: Optional - whether you consent to your name appearing on our public signatories page
  • Referral Information: If you arrived via a referral link

Pledge submissions are processed through our secure systems with Formspree as a backup service. Your data is transmitted securely and stored according to Formspree's Privacy Policy.

Your pledge data will:

  • Never be sold or shared with third parties for marketing
  • Only display your name publicly if you explicitly consent
  • Be used solely for pledge-related communications

Donation Information

When you make a donation through our website, we collect:

  • Payment Information: Processed securely by Stripe; we never see or store your full card number
  • Billing Address: Required for payment processing and tax receipt generation
  • Email Address: For sending donation receipts and tax documentation
  • Name: For donation records and acknowledgment

Donations are processed through Stripe, a PCI-compliant payment processor. Your payment information is transmitted directly to Stripe's secure servers and is subject to Stripe's Privacy Policy.

Your donation data will:

  • Be used to process your tax-deductible contribution
  • Generate receipts for your tax records
  • Never be sold or shared with third parties for marketing
  • Be retained as required for nonprofit financial record-keeping

3. How We Use Information

We use collected information to:

Improve Our Services

  • Understand which research areas are most valuable to visitors
  • Optimize website performance and user experience
  • Identify and fix technical issues
  • Develop new research tools and resources

Research and Analytics

  • Analyze usage patterns to guide research priorities
  • Measure the impact of our educational content
  • Understand our academic community and audience
  • Generate anonymized reports for research improvement

Communication

  • Respond to inquiries and support requests
  • Facilitate academic collaborations
  • Provide technical support when needed

4. Google Analytics

We use Google Analytics to understand how our website is used. Google Analytics:

  • Collects: Anonymous usage statistics and behavioral data
  • Uses Cookies: To track sessions and user interactions
  • Provides: Insights into content effectiveness and user needs
  • Retention: Data is automatically deleted after 26 months

Opt-Out Options

You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking by:

  • Installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on
  • Enabling "Do Not Track" in your browser settings
  • Using privacy-focused browsers or extensions
  • Disabling JavaScript (though this may affect site functionality)

5. Information Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell, trade, or rent your personal information. We may share information only in these limited circumstances:

Service Providers

  • Google Analytics: For website analytics (subject to their privacy policy)
  • Formspree: For processing pledge form submissions (subject to their privacy policy)
  • Stripe: For secure donation payment processing (subject to their privacy policy)
  • Hosting Providers: For website infrastructure and security

Legal Requirements

We may disclose information if required by law, legal process, or to protect:

  • Our rights and property
  • User safety and security
  • Public safety and national security
  • Investigation of fraud or illegal activities

Academic Collaboration

Aggregated, anonymized usage data may be shared in academic publications or research collaborations to advance knowledge in our field.

6. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your information:

  • Encryption: Data transmission is protected by SSL/TLS encryption
  • Access Controls: Limited access to personal data on a need-to-know basis
  • Regular Audits: Security practices are regularly reviewed and updated
  • Minimal Collection: We collect only data necessary for our stated purposes

However, no method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure. While we strive to protect your information, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

7. Data Retention

  • Analytics Data: Automatically deleted after 26 months
  • Contact Information: Retained only as long as necessary for the purpose provided
  • Research Data: Anonymized data may be retained indefinitely for research purposes
  • Legal Requirements: Some data may be retained longer if required by law

8. Your Rights and Choices

You have the following rights regarding your personal information:

Access and Correction

  • Request information about what personal data we have about you
  • Ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete information
  • Request a copy of your personal data in a portable format

Deletion and Restriction

  • Request deletion of your personal information (subject to legal requirements)
  • Ask us to restrict processing of your data
  • Object to processing based on legitimate interests

Opt-Out

  • Disable analytics tracking (see Google Analytics section above)
  • Unsubscribe from any communications (if applicable)
  • Use privacy-focused browser settings

To exercise these rights, contact us at privacy@theios.org.

9. International Users

Our services are hosted in the United States. If you access our services from outside the US, your information may be transferred to, stored, and processed in the United States.

By using our services, you consent to the transfer of your information to the United States and other countries where our service providers operate.

10. Children's Privacy

Our services are not intended for children under 13 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with personal information, please contact us immediately so we can delete such information.

11. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. Material changes will be:

  • Posted prominently on our website
  • Indicated by updating the "Last Updated" date
  • Communicated via email if we have your contact information

Your continued use of our services after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

12. Third-Party Links

Our websites may contain links to third-party sites. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of these external sites. We encourage you to read the privacy policies of any third-party sites you visit.

13. Contact Us

For questions about this Privacy Policy or our privacy practices:

Research Inquiries: research@theios.org
